Fundamental Concepts & Definitions

  • The wavefunction is the relationship between the position, \( \vec{r} \), and the probability of finding the particle there, at a given time, \( t \). \[ p_d = \Psi^* (\vec{r}, t) \Psi (\vec{r}, t) \]

  • Fourier series representation of a periodic function \( f(t) \): \[ f(t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} c_n exp(in \omega_0 t), \] where the values of \( c_n \) can be found by \[ c_n = \frac{1}{T} \int^{t_0 + T}_{t_0} exp(-in \omega_0 t) f(t) dt \]

Quantum Postulates

  • Quantum Postulate 1

    The state of a non-relativistic quantum mechanical particle at time \( t \) is specified by a continuous, single-valued, complex function \( \Psi (\vec{r}, t) \). This function, called the wavefunction or state function, has the property that \( \Psi^* (\vec{r}, t) \Psi (\vec{r}, t) d\tau \) is the probability that the particle lies in the volume element \( d\tau \) located at \( \vec{r} \) at time \( t \).
